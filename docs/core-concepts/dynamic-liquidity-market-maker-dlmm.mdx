## What is Liquidity Book?

Liquidity Book organizes liquidity into discrete price **bins** rather than a continuous curve. This enables zero-slippage trades within bins and dynamic fees.

| Aspect | Traditional AMM | Liquidity Book |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| Price | Curve ($xy=k$) | Discrete bins |
| Slippage | Every trade | Zero within bins |
| Capital efficiency | ~2-5% | Up to 100% |
| Fees | Static | Dynamic |

## How Bins Work

### Structure

Each bin holds reserves at a single price:

```
Price: $0.99  $1.00  $1.01  $1.02
        [Y]   [X+Y]   [X]    [X]
               ↑
          Active Bin
```

- **Active bin**: Contains both tokens, where trading happens
- **Left of active**: Token Y only
- **Right of active**: Token X only

### Pricing

Bins are spaced by a fixed percentage (bin step):

$$
P_{i+1} = P_i \cdot (1 + s)
$$

Example with 25bp (0.25%) step from $1.00:

| Bin | Price |
|-----|-------|
| 0 | $1.0000 |
| +1 | $1.0025 |
| +2 | $1.0050 |
| -1 | $0.9975 |

### Constant Sum Formula

Within each bin:

$$
P \cdot x + y = L
$$

Trades execute at **exact bin price**: zero slippage until the bin depletes.

## Swap Mechanics

1. Trade starts in active bin at fixed price
2. If bin depletes, trading continues in next bin
3. Large trades cross multiple bins at each bin's price

**Effective price** = weighted average of all bins touched.

## Dynamic Fees

Fees adjust automatically:

$$
\text{Total Fee} = \text{Base Fee} + \text{Variable Fee}
$$

- **Base fee**: Minimum, based on bin step
- **Variable fee**: Increases with volatility (bin crossings, swap frequency)

This creates surge pricing during volatile periods, protecting LPs.
