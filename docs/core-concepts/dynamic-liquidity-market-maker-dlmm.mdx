## What is Liquidity Book?

Liquidity Book is an automated market maker (AMM) design that organizes liquidity into discrete price bins rather than spreading it across a continuous curve.

This approach, pioneered by Trader Joe, fundamentally changes how liquidity works:

| Aspect | Continuous AMM | Liquidity Book |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| Price representation | Curve ($xy=k$) | Discrete bins |
| Liquidity distribution | Infinite range | Specific prices |
| Slippage | Every trade | Zero within bins |
| Capital efficiency | ~2-5% active | Up to 100% active |
| Fee model | Static | Dynamic |

## The Problem with Continuous Curves

Traditional AMMs use the constant product formula:

$$
x \cdot y = k
$$

This creates a smooth price curve where liquidity exists at every possible price. The problem: most of that liquidity is useless.

**Example:** A token trading at $1.00

In a constant product AMM, liquidity is spread from $0.0001 to $10,000+. But trading only happens near $1.00. Liquidity at $0.01 or $100 earns nothing — it's dead capital diluting returns for everyone.

Concentrated liquidity (Uniswap v3) improves this by letting LPs choose ranges. But it introduces complexity:
- Positions go out of range and stop earning
- Active management becomes necessary
- Gas costs for rebalancing add up

## How Discretization Works

Liquidity Book solves this differently. Instead of ranges on a curve, liquidity exists in **bins** — discrete containers at specific prices.

### Bin Structure

Each bin:
- Holds reserves at a single price
- Contains either one or both tokens
- Earns fees only when active

```
Price: $0.98  $0.99  $1.00  $1.01  $1.02
        [Y]    [Y]   [X+Y]   [X]    [X]
                       ↑
                  Active Bin
```

The **active bin** contains both tokens and is where trading occurs. Bins to the left contain only token Y (quote). Bins to the right contain only token X (base).

### Bin Pricing

Bins are spaced by a fixed percentage called the **bin step**:

$$
P_{i+1} = P_i \cdot (1 + s)
$$

Where $s$ is the bin step in decimal form.

**Example:** With a 25 basis point (0.25%) bin step starting at $1.00:

| Bin | Price |
|-----|-------|
| 0 | $1.0000 |
| 1 | $1.0025 |
| 2 | $1.0050 |
| 3 | $1.0075 |
| -1 | $0.9975 |
| -2 | $0.9950 |

### Constant Sum Within Bins

Within each bin, liquidity follows the constant sum formula:

$$
P \cdot x + y = L
$$

Where:
- $P$ = bin price
- $x$ = token X reserves
- $y$ = token Y reserves
- $L$ = total liquidity in bin

This means trades within a bin execute at **exactly** the bin price — zero slippage.

## Trading Mechanics

### Swap Execution

When a user swaps:

1. Trade starts in the active bin
2. Reserves exchange at the bin's fixed price
3. If the bin depletes, trading continues in the next bin
4. Each bin crossed adds its price to the execution

**Zero slippage scenario:** Trade fits entirely within active bin
**Multi-bin scenario:** Trade crosses bins, each at its own price

### Price Impact

Price impact only occurs when trades cross bins:

$$
\text{Effective Price} = \frac{\sum_{i} P_i \cdot \Delta x_i}{\sum_{i} \Delta x_i}
$$

Small trades in liquid bins: zero slippage.
Large trades crossing many bins: averaged price across all bins touched.

### Example Trade

Pool: ETH/USDC, Active bin at $2000, 10 bp bin step

Trade: Buy 5 ETH

| Bin | Price | ETH Available | ETH Bought | USDC Cost |
|-----|-------|---------------|------------|-----------|
| 0 | $2000 | 3 ETH | 3 ETH | $6,000 |
| 1 | $2002 | 4 ETH | 2 ETH | $4,004 |

**Result:** 5 ETH for $10,004 = $2,000.80 effective price

## Dynamic Fees

Liquidity Book implements **surge pricing** — fees that respond to market conditions.

### Fee Components

$$
\text{Total Fee} = f_b + f_v
$$

**Base Fee ($f_b$):**
$$
f_b = B \cdot s
$$

The minimum fee, determined by base factor $B$ and bin step $s$.

**Variable Fee ($f_v$):**
$$
f_v = A \cdot (v_a)^2
$$

Scales quadratically with the volatility accumulator $v_a$.

### Volatility Accumulator

The volatility accumulator tracks recent market activity:

- **Increases** with bin crossings during swaps
- **Increases** with swap frequency
- **Decays** over time when activity slows

This creates automatic surge pricing:
- Quiet markets → low fees
- Active/volatile markets → high fees

### Why Dynamic Fees Matter

**For LPs:**
- Higher compensation during risky periods
- Protection against toxic flow

**For Traders:**
- Lower costs during normal conditions
- Discourages extractive strategies during volatility

**For the Market:**
- Natural equilibrium between LP risk and trader cost
- Reduced manipulation incentives

## LP Positions

### Position Structure

LP positions in Liquidity Book are represented by shares in specific bins:

```
Position: [
  { binId: 8388608, shares: 1000000 },
  { binId: 8388609, shares: 500000 },
  { binId: 8388610, shares: 500000 }
]
```

Each bin has its own share tracking, similar to ERC-1155 tokens.

### Adding Liquidity

LPs can add liquidity to:
- Single bins (concentrated, higher risk/reward)
- Multiple bins (distributed, lower risk/reward)
- Custom distributions (strategy-specific)

### Liquidity Shapes

**Uniform:** Equal distribution across bins
```
[====][====][====][====][====]
```

**Concentrated:** Heavy in active bin, light on edges
```
[==][====][========][====][==]
```

**Bid-Ask:** Liquidity on sides, gap in middle
```
[========][====][  ][====][========]
```

### Fee Earning

Only the active bin earns fees. As price moves:
- Bins become active/inactive
- Fee earning shifts accordingly
- LPs in active bins capture all trading fees

### Impermanent Loss

IL in Liquidity Book differs from continuous AMMs:

- **Within bin:** No IL (constant sum)
- **Across bins:** Discrete IL as bins flip

Concentrated positions have higher IL risk but higher fee potential. Distributed positions have lower IL but diluted fee capture.

| Feature | Uniswap v2 | Uniswap v3 | Liquidity Book |
|---------|------------|------------|----------------|
| Curve | $xy=k$ | $xy=k$ (bounded) | $Px+y=L$ per bin |
| Slippage | Always | Always | Zero in-bin |
| Positions | Fungible | NFT | Semi-fungible |

| Complexity | Low | High | Medium |
