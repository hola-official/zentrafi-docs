## The Integration Thesis

Most protocols treat launches and trading as separate problems. Launch on one platform, list on another, hope they connect.

ZentraFi rejects this separation. Zentra App (launchpad) and Zentra X (DEX) are designed as a single system where each phase flows into the next.

## The Complete Flow

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        LAUNCH PHASE                                  │
│  Project configures → Users participate → Price discovers           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                                  │
                                  ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                      TRANSITION PHASE                                │
│  Launch closes → Liquidity seeds → Bins populate                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                                  │
                                  ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                       TRADING PHASE                                  │
│  Markets open → DLMM manages liquidity → Ongoing price discovery    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Phase 1: Adaptive Distribution

When a project launches on Zentra App, they configure:

- **Token parameters**: Supply, name, metadata
- **Pricing curve**: How price responds to demand
- **Duration**: How long the launch runs
- **Liquidity allocation**: What percentage seeds the market

Participants buy tokens through adaptive pricing. Early buyers get lower prices. As demand increases, so does the price. This mechanism:

- Rewards early participation without punishing late arrivals
- Discovers a market-clearing price organically
- Prevents bot frontrunning through continuous pricing

## Phase 2: Liquidity Formation

When the launch completes:

1. **Raised capital is allocated**: A portion (defined by the creator) goes to liquidity
2. **Bins are populated**: Liquidity seeds into DLMM bins around the terminal price
3. **Pool goes live**: Trading activates on Zentra X

This happens atomically. There's no gap where the token exists without a market.

### Why This Matters

Traditional launches create a liquidity bootstrapping problem:

```
Launch ends → ??? → Trading begins
```

That "???" is where most projects struggle. They need to:
- Source capital for liquidity (often from team allocations)
- Choose an AMM and configure pools
- Hope initial trading doesn't drain their LP

ZentraFi eliminates this gap. Launch capital becomes trading liquidity. The transition is automatic.

## Phase 3: DLMM Trading

Once live on Zentra X, the token trades through the Liquidity Book model:

### Bin-Based Execution

Liquidity exists in discrete price bins. Each bin:
- Holds reserves at a fixed price
- Executes trades at zero slippage until depleted
- Moves the market to the next bin when exhausted

### Dynamic Fees

Fees respond to market conditions:
- **Base fee**: Minimum cost set by pool configuration
- **Variable fee**: Scales with volatility (bin crossings, swap frequency)

During launch days and high-volatility periods, fees increase automatically. This:
- Compensates LPs for increased risk
- Discourages extractive trading strategies
- Captures more value during peak demand

### Natural Liquidity Management

Because initial liquidity is seeded around the launch price, the market starts with concentrated depth. As trading continues:

- Price movement depletes bins on one side
- LPs can rebalance or add to positions
- Additional liquidity providers join as the market matures

## Technical Details

### Liquidity Seeding Parameters

Pool creators configure:
- **Bin step**: Price increment between bins (e.g., 0.25%)
- **Active bin range**: How many bins around the active price receive liquidity
- **Distribution shape**: Uniform, concentrated, or custom

### Price Continuity

The final launch price determines the initial active bin:

$$
\text{Active Bin} = \text{floor}\left(\frac{\log(\text{Launch Price})}{\log(1 + \text{Bin Step})}\right)
$$

This ensures trading begins at exactly the price where distribution ended.

### Liquidity Distribution

Initial liquidity can follow different distributions:

- **Uniform**: Equal amounts across all seeded bins
- **Concentrated**: Heavier allocation near the active bin
- **Custom**: Project-defined distribution for specific use cases
