## The Fragmentation Problem

DeFi infrastructure evolved in silos. Launchpads, DEXs, and liquidity protocols each optimized for their specific function without considering how tokens actually move through the ecosystem.

The result: **friction at every handoff**.

### Launch → Liquidity Gap

A project launches on Platform A. Launch completes. Now what?

They need to:
1. Withdraw raised funds
2. Create a liquidity pool on Platform B
3. Seed it with their own capital (often locked in vesting)
4. Hope enough trading activity develops before incentives run dry

Most projects fail somewhere in this sequence. Not because of bad products, but because the infrastructure makes bootstrapping unnecessarily hard.

### Liquidity → Trading Disconnect

Even when liquidity exists, traditional AMMs waste it.

Constant-product AMMs (Uniswap v2 style) spread liquidity across all possible prices. If a token trades at $1, liquidity sitting at $0.01 or $100 contributes nothing — it's dead capital that dilutes fee earnings for everyone.

Concentrated liquidity (Uniswap v3 style) improves this, but introduces complexity that most users can't manage effectively. Positions go out of range. Rebalancing costs gas. Active management becomes a full-time job.

### The Coordination Failure

Without coordination, ecosystem development becomes a prisoner's dilemma:

- Projects hoard tokens for liquidity mining instead of building
- LPs chase short-term yields instead of supporting useful markets
- Traders fragment across platforms, reducing depth everywhere

Everyone acts rationally. Everyone ends up worse off.

## What Pharos Needs

Pharos is building toward high-throughput, low-cost infrastructure. This creates opportunity — but also requirements:

**Speed demands efficiency.** Fast blocks mean more trading activity. More activity needs deeper liquidity. Deep liquidity requires capital-efficient AMM design.

**Low costs enable experimentation.** Cheap transactions mean more projects can launch. More launches need reliable market formation. Reliable markets need integrated infrastructure.

**RWA focus demands stability.** Real-world asset tokenization requires predictable execution. Predictable execution needs discretized pricing. Discretized pricing is what Liquidity Book provides.
