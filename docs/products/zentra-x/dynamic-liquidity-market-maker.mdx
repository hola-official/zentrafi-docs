## Overview

Liquidity provision on Zentra X uses the Liquidity Book model. Instead of depositing into a single pool position, you allocate capital across specific price bins.

This guide covers:
- How LP positions work
- Choosing liquidity shapes
- Managing and rebalancing positions
- Understanding risks and rewards

## How LP Positions Work

### Bin-Based Allocation

When you provide liquidity, you're depositing into one or more price bins:

```
Your Position:
├── Bin 8388600 ($1.95): 500 USDC worth
├── Bin 8388608 ($2.00): 1000 USDC worth  ← Active bin
├── Bin 8388616 ($2.05): 500 USDC worth
└── Total: 2000 USDC equivalent
```

Each bin is tracked separately. You receive shares proportional to your contribution in each bin.

### Token Composition

Your deposit composition depends on which bins you're adding to:

| Bin Location | You Deposit | Why |
|--------------|-------------|-----|
| Below active | Token Y only | These bins are "sold out" of X |
| Active bin | Both tokens | Mixed composition |
| Above active | Token X only | These bins are "sold out" of Y |

**Example:** Adding to bins around $2.00 ETH/USDC

- Bins at $1.95, $1.98: Deposit USDC only
- Bin at $2.00 (active): Deposit ETH + USDC
- Bins at $2.02, $2.05: Deposit ETH only

### Fee Earning

Only the **active bin** earns trading fees. Your earnings depend on:

1. **Whether price is in your bins** — Out-of-range bins earn nothing
2. **Your share of active bin** — Proportional to your liquidity share
3. **Trading volume** — More trades = more fees
4. **Fee level** — Dynamic fees during volatility

## Liquidity Shapes

Zentra X offers preset shapes for common strategies:

### Spot (Uniform)

```
Distribution:  [====][====][====][====][====]
Risk Level:    Medium
Best For:      Ranging markets, passive LPs
```

Equal liquidity across all selected bins.

**Pros:**
- Balanced exposure
- Consistent fee earning across price range
- Lower rebalancing needs

**Cons:**
- Diluted fee capture (spread thin)
- Not optimal for trending markets

### Curve (Concentrated)

```
Distribution:  [==][====][========][====][==]
Risk Level:    Higher
Best For:      Stable pairs, high conviction
```

Heavy allocation to active bin, tapering outward.

**Pros:**
- Maximum fee capture at current price
- Highest capital efficiency

**Cons:**
- Higher IL if price moves
- Requires more active management

### Bid-Ask

```
Distribution:  [========][====][  ][====][========]
Risk Level:    Lower
Best For:      Volatility capture, mean reversion
```

Liquidity on both sides with gap around active price.

**Pros:**
- Captures price swings
- Natural DCA effect
- Lower IL in range-bound markets

**Cons:**
- Misses fees during stable periods
- Complex rebalancing

## Adding Liquidity

### Step-by-Step

1. **Select Pool**
   - Choose token pair (e.g., PHRS/USDC)
   - Review pool stats (volume, TVL, fees)

2. **Choose Strategy**
   - Select liquidity shape
   - Set price range (bin range)
   - Review token requirements

3. **Deposit Tokens**
   - Approve token spending
   - Confirm deposit transaction
   - Receive LP position

### Configuration Options

| Parameter | Description | Impact |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Bin Range | How many bins to cover | Wider = more coverage, less concentration |
| Shape | Distribution pattern | Affects fee capture and IL |
| Amount | Total value to deposit | Proportional to your share |

## Managing Positions

### Monitoring

Track your positions through the Zentra X interface:

- **Current value:** Real-time valuation
- **Fees earned:** Accumulated and claimable
- **In-range status:** Whether you're earning
- **Bin distribution:** Visual representation

### Rebalancing

When price moves significantly, consider rebalancing:

**Signs you need to rebalance:**
- Position mostly out of range
- Accumulated IL exceeds fee earnings
- Market regime changed

**How to rebalance:**
1. Withdraw from current position
2. Re-add at new price range
3. Consider transaction costs vs. benefit

### Claiming Fees

Fees accumulate in your position and can be claimed anytime:

- No lockup or vesting
- Claimed in the tokens you're LPing
- Gas cost for claim transaction

## Risk Management

### Impermanent Loss

IL in Liquidity Book works differently than continuous AMMs:

**Within a bin:** No IL. Constant sum means reserves change but value doesn't.

**Across bins:** Discrete IL when bins flip. Each bin crossing is like a limit order filling.

**IL Formula (simplified):**
$$
\text{IL} = 2 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{r}}{1+r} - 1
$$

Where $r$ = price ratio (current/initial).

### Concentrated Position Risk

More concentration = higher risk/reward:

| Concentration | Fee Potential | IL Risk | Management Need |
|---------------|---------------|---------|-----------------|
| Wide (many bins) | Lower | Lower | Passive |
| Medium | Moderate | Moderate | Occasional |
| Tight (few bins) | Higher | Higher | Active |

### Market Conditions

Different markets favor different strategies:

| Market Type | Recommended Shape | Rationale |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Stable pair | Tight curve | Price stays in range |
| Volatile pair | Wide spot | Price moves unpredictably |
| Trending | Bid-ask or single-side | Capture directional moves |
| Launching token | Curve near launch price | High volume, concentrated trading |

## Advanced Strategies

### Single-Sided Liquidity

Deposit only one token by selecting bins on one side:

**Selling strategy (above current price):**
- Deposit token X in bins above active
- As price rises, X sells for Y
- Natural take-profit mechanism

**Buying strategy (below current price):**
- Deposit token Y in bins below active
- As price falls, Y buys X
- Natural DCA mechanism

### Range Orders

Use tight bin ranges as limit orders:

1. Place liquidity in single bin above/below current price
2. When price reaches that bin, trade executes
3. Position flips to opposite token

This creates on-chain limit orders without active monitoring.

### Multi-Position Management

Advanced LPs run multiple positions:

- Core position: Wide, passive
- Satellite positions: Tight, actively managed
- Hedging positions: Opposite side exposure

## Common Mistakes

<Warning>
**Avoid these common LP errors:**

1. **Too concentrated without monitoring** — Tight positions need attention
2. **Ignoring IL during trends** — Rebalance before losses compound
3. **Chasing high APRs** — Volume-based APRs are backward-looking
4. **Over-trading** — Transaction costs eat into profits
</Warning>
