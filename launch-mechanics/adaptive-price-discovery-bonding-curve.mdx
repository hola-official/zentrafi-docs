## The Price Discovery Problem

Traditional token launches fail in predictable ways:

| Launch Type | Problem |
|-------------|---------|
| Fixed price | Bots front-run, insiders get allocations |
| First-come-first-served | Gas wars, failed transactions |
| Whitelist | Centralized, excludes most participants |
| Dutch auction | Complex, often ends at floor price |

The common failure: **price is set before demand is known**.

When launch price doesn't reflect actual demand, someone captures the difference: usually bots, insiders, or early flippers rather than genuine participants.

## Adaptive Pricing

ZentraFi's launchpad uses **adaptive price discovery**: the price responds to demand in real-time throughout the launch.

### Core Mechanism

$$
P(t) = P_0 \cdot f(S(t))
$$

Where:
- $P(t)$ = Current price
- $P_0$ = Starting price
- $S(t)$ = Tokens sold so far
- $f$ = Pricing function

As more tokens sell, price increases. Early participants get better prices, but late participants aren't excluded: they simply pay market-discovered prices.

### Why This Works

**No fixed allocations to game:**
Everyone buys at the current price. No racing to claim spots.

**Real-time price discovery:**
Final price reflects actual demand, not team estimates.

**Continuous participation:**
Buy anytime during the launch at the current price.

**Bot resistance:**
No advantage to speed: only to price tolerance.

## Bonding Curve Types

### Linear Curve

$$
P(S) = P_0 + k \cdot S
$$

Price increases by fixed amount per token sold.

```
Price
  │        ╱
  │      ╱
  │    ╱
  │  ╱
  │╱
  └─────────── Tokens Sold
```

**Characteristics:**
- Predictable price increase
- Moderate early advantage
- Simple to understand

**Best for:** Community tokens, fair launches

### Exponential Curve

$$
P(S) = P_0 \cdot e^{k \cdot S}
$$

Price increases faster as more tokens sell.

```
Price
  │           │
  │          ╱
  │        ╱
  │      ╱
  │____╱
  └─────────── Tokens Sold
```

**Characteristics:**
- Strong early participant advantage
- Rapid price acceleration
- Natural supply scarcity

**Best for:** High-demand launches, price discovery focus

### Sigmoid Curve

$$
P(S) = P_0 + \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(S - S_0)}}
$$

S-shaped curve with slow start, rapid middle, and plateau.

```
Price
  │         ____
  │       ╱
  │     ╱
  │   ╱
  │__╱
  └─────────── Tokens Sold
```

**Characteristics:**
- Gentle start encourages early participation
- Rapid discovery phase in middle
- Price ceiling prevents extreme costs

**Best for:** Balanced launches, controlled price ranges

## Launch Configuration

### Parameters

| Parameter | Description | Impact |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| `startPrice` | Initial token price | Floor for all purchases |
| `curveType` | Linear/Exponential/Sigmoid | Price behavior |
| `curveParameter` | Steepness factor | How fast price rises |
| `softCap` | Minimum raise target | Launch validity threshold |
| `hardCap` | Maximum raise amount | Launch completion trigger |
| `duration` | Launch window | Time available to participate |
| `liquidityAllocation` | % to seed pools | Post-launch market depth |

### Example Configuration

```
Token: PROJECT
Curve: Exponential
Start Price: $0.001
Curve Parameter: 0.00001
Soft Cap: $50,000
Hard Cap: $500,000
Duration: 48 hours
Liquidity: 80%
```

**Expected behavior:**
- First 10% of tokens: ~$0.001-0.002
- Middle 50%: ~$0.002-0.01
- Final 10%: ~$0.01-0.05+

2. **Review launch details**: curve, caps, allocation

4. **See estimated tokens** based on current price
5. **Confirm transaction**

### Price Execution

Your purchase executes along the curve:

**Small purchase:** Mostly at displayed price
**Large purchase:** Averages across price increase

$$
\text{Tokens Received} = \int_{S_0}^{S_1} \frac{1}{P(S)} dS
$$

Where $S_0$ is tokens sold before your purchase, $S_1$ is after.

### Example Purchase

Launch state: 1M tokens sold at $0.005 current price

You buy with $1,000:

| Tokens Bought | Avg Price | Cost |
|---------------|-----------|------|
| 0-50,000 | $0.0050 | $250 |
| 50,000-100,000 | $0.0052 | $260 |
| 100,000-150,000 | $0.0054 | $270 |
| 150,000-180,000 | $0.0056 | $220 |

**Result:** 180,000 tokens for $1,000 = $0.00556 average

## Anti-Gaming Mechanisms

### No Speed Advantage

Adaptive pricing removes first-mover advantages:
- Price is the same for everyone at any moment
- No benefit to faster transactions
- Gas optimization doesn't help

### Continuous Clearing

Unlike batch auctions, prices clear continuously:
- No hidden order books
- Transparent current price
- Immediate execution certainty

### Economic Alignment

The curve creates natural incentives:
- Early = lower price, but less information
- Late = higher price, but proven demand
- Manipulation = buy your own curve higher

## Integration with DLMM

### Liquidity Seeding

When launch completes:

1. **Calculate terminal price** from curve endpoint
2. **Allocate liquidity portion** (e.g., 80% of raise)
3. **Determine active bin** matching terminal price
4. **Distribute across bins** per configured shape
5. **Activate pool** on Zentra X

### Price Continuity

The terminal launch price becomes the initial trading price:

$$
\text{Initial Active Bin} = \text{floor}\left(\frac{\log(P_{terminal})}{\log(1 + s)}\right)
$$

No gap between launch price and first trade price.

### Liquidity Shape

Initial liquidity typically uses concentrated distribution:
- Heavy allocation at terminal price bin
- Tapering allocation in adjacent bins
- Supports immediate trading depth
