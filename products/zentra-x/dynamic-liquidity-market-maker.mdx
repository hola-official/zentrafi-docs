## How You Earn

### The Active Bin Rule

Only the **active bin** earns trading fees. Your earnings depend on:

```
Your Fee Share = (Your Liquidity in Active Bin / Total Bin Liquidity) × Fees
```

**Practical example:**

```
Pool: ETH/USDC
Active bin: $2,000
Total bin liquidity: $100,000
Your contribution: $5,000 (5%)

Trade happens: 10 ETH bought ($20,000 volume)
Fee rate: 0.3%
Total fees: $60

Your earnings: $60 × 5% = $3.00
```

### Why Concentration Matters

| Strategy | Your $10,000 | Fees on $100k volume |
|----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Spread across 100 bins | $100/bin | ~$0.30 |
| Concentrated in 10 bins | $1,000/bin | ~$3.00 |
| Concentrated in 3 bins | $3,333/bin | ~$10.00 |

Same capital. Same volume. **33x more fees** when concentrated.

## Managing Your Position

### When to Rebalance

**Consider rebalancing when:**
- Position is 100% one token (fully out of range)
- More than 50% of position is out of range
- Market conditions fundamentally changed

**Don't panic rebalance when:**
- Position temporarily out of range (it may return)
- Small movements in/out of range
- You're just checking because you're bored

### Rebalancing Process

1. **Assess**: Is rebalancing worth the gas cost?
2. **Withdraw**: Remove liquidity from current bins
3. **Reposition**: Add to new bins around current price
4. **Confirm**: Check new position is in range

<Warning>
**Gas costs matter.** Rebalancing a $1,000 position that's slightly out of range for $0.50 in fees doesn't make sense. Be patient.
</Warning>

### Claiming Fees

- Fees accrue automatically
- Claim anytime (no lockup)
- Paid in the pool's tokens
- Consider claiming when fees > gas cost

## Risk Factors

### Impermanent Loss (IL)

IL happens when price moves away from your entry. In DLMM:

- **Within a bin**: Zero IL (constant sum formula)
- **Across bins**: Discrete IL when bins flip

**IL mitigation strategies:**
1. Use wider ranges (lower IL, lower fees)
2. Choose less volatile pairs
3. Accept IL as the cost of earning fees
4. Use Bid-Ask shape to catch swings

### The Idle Position Problem

If price moves outside your range:
- You stop earning fees
- Your position becomes 100% one token
- You're waiting for price to return

**This is why shape and range matter.** Too tight = more idle time.

### Volatility Risk

During volatile periods:
- More bin crossings = more IL
- BUT: Dynamic fees increase, compensating you
- Net result: You're protected compared to traditional AMMs

## Quick Tips

### Do

- Start with a moderate range until you understand the mechanics
- Check your position daily if using concentrated strategies
- Use dynamic fees to your advantage during volatility
- Claim fees regularly if amounts are significant

### Don't

- Go maximum concentration without monitoring capability
- Ignore IL in trending markets (fees may not compensate)
- Over-trade (gas costs compound)
- Chase the highest APR without understanding the risk

<Warning>
**Common mistakes:**
- Setting 3-bin range and forgetting to check for a week
- Rebalancing every time position goes slightly out of range
- Ignoring that your position is now 95% MEME token and 5% USDC
</Warning>
